Welcome to Safeguarding Awareness

Safeguarding is about protecting people’s health, well-being, and human rights to keep them safe from harm, abuse, or neglect. It is especially important for children, young people, and vulnerable adults. Safeguarding helps prevent abuse and creates safe, caring environments where everyone feels respected and supported.

Key information

Learn the essential steps to identify, act, and report safeguarding concerns effectively.

What is safeguarding?

Safeguarding means protecting people from harm, abuse, and neglect.

  • Safeguarding helps keep children, young people, and vulnerable adults safe from abuse and neglect.
  • It ensures everyone feels secure, respected, and supported in their surroundings.
  • Safeguarding helps individuals live, learn, and grow safely and confidently.
Signs to watch

Be aware of signs that someone may be at risk. Recognising early warning signs can save lives.

  • Notice unexplained injuries or emotional withdrawal.
  • Look for neglect, fear, or sudden behavior changes.
  • Trust your instincts — if something feels wrong, act.
Action Steps

Reporting concerns ensures that trained professionals can provide protection and support.

  • Contact your Designated Safeguarding Lead immediately.
  • Follow your organization’s reporting policy.
  • In emergencies, call emergency services (999 in the UK).

Take Action

  • Understand your safeguarding responsibilities.
  • Stay up-to-date with safeguarding training.
  • Follow the correct process for reporting incidents.
  • Promote a culture of safety and awareness.
