Key Information

Learn to recognise, act, and report safeguarding concerns confidently.

What is Safeguarding?

Safeguarding means protecting people from harm, abuse, and neglect.

  • Ensures safety, dignity, and well-being.
  • Helps build trust and awareness.
  • Applies to all, especially vulnerable groups.
Signs to Watch

Look for behavioural or physical signs that may indicate abuse or neglect.

  • Unexplained injuries or emotional changes.
  • Neglect, withdrawal, or fearfulness.
  • Listen, observe, and act if concerned.
Reporting

Reporting concerns ensures trained professionals can act quickly to protect individuals.

  • Report immediately to your Safeguarding Lead.
  • Document facts clearly and calmly.
  • Contact emergency services if in danger.

Take Action

  • Stay informed and complete safeguarding training regularly.
  • Know your reporting policy and procedures.
  • Encourage open conversations about safety.
  • Be proactive — if in doubt, report it.
